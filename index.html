Master's Project: Zinc Oxide Nanocrystals Under Annealing at Different Temperatures

Abstract We present here the results of studies on the structure and bonding of Zinc Oxide (ZnO) nanocrystals prepared by co-precipitation method at different annealing temperatures varying from as prepared to 600◦C. We have used X-ray diffraction (XRD) for structural studies and Raman spectroscopy (micro-Raman) for study of bonding. The quality of crystal is found to be excellent and the ordering is steadily enhanced with annealing temperature. There is shrinkage of unit cell volume with temperature, the volume reaching minimum at 500◦C, with preferred shrinking normal to the c-axis and c/a ratio reaching its maximum at the same temperature. Raman spectra indicates a flip in the intensity of the Zn-Zn and O-O bonds normal to the c-axis corresponding to an ordering of the O-sublattice at 500◦C. Concurrently there is a transition from bulk defects to surface defects and a change from local to global ordering.
